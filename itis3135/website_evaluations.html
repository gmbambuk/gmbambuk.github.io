Website Evaluations

The CRAP principles of design (Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity) help designers create websites that are visually appealing, organized, and easy to use. The following evaluations examine one website that demonstrates these principles effectively and another that struggles to apply them consistently.

Apple

  • Contrast: Apple uses contrast intentionally to direct the user's attention. Large product images stand out against clean backgrounds, while typography uses size and weight differences to establish a clear visual hierarchy.
  • Repetition: Consistent fonts, spacing, button styles, and color choices are repeated throughout the site. This creates a cohesive brand identity and helps users understand how to navigate the site.
  • Alignment: Content is carefully aligned using a grid-based layout. Text, images, and interactive elements appear balanced and organized, giving the site a professional appearance.
  • Proximity: Related information is grouped together effectively. Product descriptions, specifications, and calls to action are placed close to one another, reducing confusion and improving usability.

Ling's Cars

  • Contrast: While the website certainly attracts attention, it uses so many bright colors, flashing graphics, and competing visual elements that users may struggle to determine what is most important.
  • Repetition: Some branding elements are repeated, but the site often feels inconsistent because numerous fonts, images, animations, and decorative elements compete for attention at the same time.
  • Alignment: Many page elements appear crowded together or placed in unusual locations. This creates visual clutter and makes the page more difficult to scan quickly.
  • Proximity: Information is frequently packed into dense sections. Because related content is not always grouped clearly, users may need to spend additional time searching for information.
